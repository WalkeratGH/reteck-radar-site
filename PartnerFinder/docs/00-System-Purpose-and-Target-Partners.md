## In one sentence

This system is a **partner search-and-scoring database** that helps us
systematically find and rank **IT / AI Infrastructure System Integrators
(SI Suppliers)** — especially those who, in their routine **equipment leasing**
and services to **small & medium enterprises (SMEs)**, consume **large volumes of
GPUs, RAM, and servers**.

---

## Business background: why Re-Teck wants these companies

Re-Teck's core is **ITAD / data center asset disposition and hardware reuse**
(recovery, refurbishment, second-life resale). So we care most about companies that
**consume hardware in large, continuous volumes** — such companies are valuable to
Re-Teck in two ways:

1. **Demand side (buyer / supply target):** when they run equipment leasing or build
   IT / AI environments for SMEs, they need batch after batch of GPUs, RAM, and
   servers. Re-Teck can **supply refurbished / second-life hardware** to lower their
   cost.
2. **Channel side (partner):** they are a channel that touches many SMEs, so they can
   become a delivery partner for Re-Teck's hardware and services; the old equipment
   they retire is also a **source of supply for Re-Teck's recycling**.

In short: **they are constantly buying hardware and constantly swapping it out —
which fits both the supply and the recovery ends of Re-Teck's business.**

---

## What the ideal target company looks like

**Core type:** IT / AI Infrastructure System Integrator, data center service vendor,
AI compute builder.

**Positive signals (the more, the better):**

- Offers **equipment leasing / Hardware-as-a-Service (HaaS)**.
- Serves mainly **SMEs**, delivering "build it, ship it, run it" turnkey solutions.
- Builds **AI servers / GPU workstations**, or deploys **Edge AI / local LLMs**.
- Clearly needs **GPUs (NVIDIA / AMD)** and **large amounts of RAM / storage**.
- Has hands-on capability: **Data Center Smart Hands, IMAC, Break/Fix, cooling &
  power planning**.
- Is a **Microsoft / Dell / Cisco / HPE** partner (signals volume and trust).

**All of these map to "capability fields" and "scoring items" in the system** — the
more that are checked, the higher the score, and the system automatically ranks it
as **Level A (contact first)**.

## How this system helps us find them

1. **Keyword Generator:** enter country / city / service type and it auto-produces
   search phrases like "GPU workstation provider Austin", "AI server builder Texas",
   "on-prem AI deployment company Dell NVIDIA partner".
2. **Web Search (automatic):** send a phrase straight to the web (Brave API), list
   candidate companies, file with one click.
3. **Create + capability checklist:** record the company's IT / Data Center / AI
   capabilities item by item (incl. GPU, RAM/storage, AI servers, Edge AI, cooling
   & power).
4. **Automatic scoring (100 pts) + grading (A/B/C):** the system scores by
   capability — A = contact first, B = confirm further, C = on hold.
5. **Dashboard / Export:** see the overview at a glance (by country, AI-capable
   count, to-contact, etc.) and export to CSV / Excel for follow-up.

## Mini glossary (for non-technical readers)

- **SI (System Integrator):** a company that assembles servers, networking, and
  software into a complete solution for the customer.
- **GPU:** graphics/AI compute chip (e.g. NVIDIA); required to run AI, expensive,
  high demand.
- **RAM (memory):** a computer's/server's working memory; AI and virtualization
  need lots of it.
- **Edge AI:** running AI on on-site devices instead of in the cloud.
- **Local LLM / On-Prem AI:** AI / large language models running in the customer's
  own facility.
- **Smart Hands / IMAC / Break-Fix:** on-site data center labor — installation,
  moves, and fault repair.
- **Equipment Leasing:** customers rent rather than buy, so the supplier must keep
  restocking hardware.
- **SME / SMB:** small & medium (or small & midsize) business.
